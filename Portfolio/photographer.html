HOW I BECAME A PHOTOGRAPHER


  • by: Adamu Aliyu
  • date: Jan 17, 2024
  • 2 Comments

From a young age, i was captivated by the sight of people walking around with cameras. I felt a strong desire to be like them "Photographers".
When i was a child, my dad bought a camera. It wasn't really intended for me, but rather for the family, so we wouldn't always have to call a photographer when we needed to take pictures.
Despite this, i would often sneak into his room when he wasn't around, take the camera, and start shooting landscape photos. No one really taught me how to use the camera, but i gradually figured it out on my own
After a while, the camera broke and as i grew older, i got a phone. Even though it only had an 8MP camera, I actually used it to take pictures. PicsArt quickly became-and still is-my favorite editing tool. I would take photos that weren't perfect, but after editing them in PicsArt, adjusting the resolution and making enhacements, they would look just right. That's how i got started.

And so, eventually, i bought a camera. While not many people know about me being a photographer, it's not like i took it as a work. It's just that, over the years i've developed a prefernce for shooting landscape, animals, skies, moon, objects and other natural scenes. I don't particularly enjoy taking pictures of people though, but yeah... I do sometimes when necessary. I just simply prefer capturing objects and the beauty around me.
So, here's what i realized... The equipment doesn't really matter and it doesn't define the photographer. While i started with a basic phone camera, i was still able to to create images that i was proud of. It is the passion and the creativity behind the lens that truly matter.
So, as we continue to grow and improve, i hope we keep exploring new subjects, learning new things and techniues, and capturing the world in that way that resonates with others.
Photography, for me, is a lifelng journey of discovery and expression.

Start with the things you have... No matter how small it may be. What really matters in this world is experience and also the skill, so don't always complain, and don't let go of those dreams.

If you really want to be a successful person, then you've to start with the baby steps. So, as long as you have the passion, just start small and you are going to be surprised of what awaits you on the other side.
